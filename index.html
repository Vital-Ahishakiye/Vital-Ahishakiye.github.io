Data Science

Rwanda: Understanding Unemployment Drivers

Personal project: Leveraging 2022 Census data, I analyzed demographic, household, economic, and education factors to identify key drivers of unemployment in Rwanda. Results revealed that over 60% of tertiary-educated individuals are employed, while digital connectivity and urban location significantly impact employability. Critically, 95% of people in temporary camps or settlements face unemployment, making them Rwanda's most vulnerable group requiring urgent employment and social protection programs.

Rwanda: Public Sentiment Analysis of Distance-Based Fare Pricing Model

Hackathon: In December 2024, RURA introduced distance-based fares allowing proportional payment based on travel distance. During a hackathon organized by Risa and Rwanda ICT Chamber, I analyzed public perception of this policy. Analysis revealed ~50% of comments were neutral or confused, indicating limited understanding. The key recommendation: future policy rollouts must include clear, accessible public education to build trust and ensure smoother adoption.

West Africa: Automating Palm Tree Counting

Client project: A West African client previously used GIS applications and manual counting to inventory palm trees across large hectares from high-resolution aerial imagery. I developed a computer vision solution that reduced counting time from 12 minutes to under 1 second per image, dramatically improving efficiency and accuracy.

Geospatial Analysis

Rwanda: Pre-Primary Education Accessibility

Personal project: Following a March 2021 UNICEF report showing 33% of Primary 3 students cannot read grade-level Kinyarwanda text (and 90% struggle with English), I analyzed 2022 education data to test whether inadequate pre-primary infrastructure contributes to these learning gaps. Mapping student-to-school ratios across Rwanda's 416 sectors revealed that 71% lack pre-primary schools, highlighting critical infrastructure deficiencies. While the NST1 (2017-2024) featured Early Childhood Development prominently under Social Transformation, significant expansion is still needed to build the foundation for a skilled and productive population.

Pittsburgh: Health Transportation to Work

Class project: Using U.S. Census American Community Survey data (2014-2019), I visualized walking and biking commute patterns across Pittsburgh to support the city's Complete Streets policy implementation. Analysis revealed that walking commuters are concentrated in specific neighborhoods rather than distributed evenly citywide. Key factors driving this pattern include multiple employment centers (Central Business District, Oakland, East Liberty) and Pittsburgh's transformation into an education and healthcare hub. These insights help city planners identify priority areas for pedestrian infrastructure investment.
