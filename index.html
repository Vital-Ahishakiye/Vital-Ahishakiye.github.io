Outstanding Projects

  • I. Accessibility and Quality in Rwanda's Education System

    In January 2024, UNICEF reported that a significant number of Rwandan children complete primary education without solid foundational skills. Specifically, 33% of Primary 3 class students are unable to read and understand a grade-level text in Kinyarwanda, and nearly 90% are unable to do so in English, the official language of instruction. This issue is rooted in early childhood education challenges. UNICEF

    Analyzing census data and information on Rwandan schools, I assessed the student-to-school ratio across the country's 416 sectors. The findings revealed that 298 sectors lack any early childhood education facilities, and 4 sectors have no primary schools, indicating that approximately 71% of sectors are without early childhood education infrastructure.

    Overcrowding is a significant concern in primary schools, with 36% of sectors having an average of 1,500 students per school. Given that 40% of Rwanda's population is under 15 years old, overcrowding and limited accessibility to primary and pre-primary education remain pressing issues. Addressing these challenges is crucial for the country's future, as quality education is a key contributor to employment and overall development. GOV.UK ASSETS
